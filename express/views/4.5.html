A wind turbine is an electricity generator on top of a tall tower. The generator is driven by the force of the wind on its blades.

A wave generator at a coastal site uses the motion of waves to make a floating section move up and down. This motion drives a turbine which turns a generator.

A cable between the generator and the shore delivers electricity to local users or via a network of cables (referred to as the grid system) to distant users.

Hydroelectricity is generated when rain water collected in an uphill reservoir flows downhill. The water flow drives turbines that turn electricity generators at the foot of the hill.

A tidal power station in an estuary traps each high tide behind a barrage. The high tide is then released into the sea through turbines that drive generators in the barrage.
